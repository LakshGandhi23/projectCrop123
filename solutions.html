Solutions

MSP for all crops

The government can consider implementing a system of MSP for all crops. This would provide a guaranteed price to farmers for their produce, thereby protecting them from price fluctuations and exploitation by middlemen. The MSP can be determined based on a formula that considers the cost of production, a minimum profit margin, and other relevant factors.

Debt relief

The government can introduce schemes to provide debt relief to farmers. This could involve loan waivers, restructuring of loans, or providing subsidies on interest payments. This would help to alleviate the financial burden on farmers and improve their overall financial viability.

Pension for farmers and farm laborers

The government can establish a pension scheme for farmers and farm laborers. This would provide them with a source of income in their old age and improve their social security.

Investment in rural infrastructure

The government can increase investment in rural infrastructure, such as irrigation facilities, roads, and storage facilities. This would improve the efficiency of agricultural production and reduce post-harvest losses.

Promoting Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs)

The government can encourage the formation of FPOs, which are groups of farmers who come together to improve their bargaining power and access to markets. This would help farmers to get better prices for their produce and reduce their dependence on middlemen.

MSP calculation based on C2 cost

MSP currently calculated as A2 + FL cost x 1.5 by CACP. Abhijit Sen's committee proposes MSP calculation based on C2 cost including Owned Capital and Land Rental Value.

Transparency and communication

The government can improve transparency and communication with farmers by regularly consulting with them on agricultural policies and ensuring that their concerns are addressed. This would help to build trust and cooperation between the government and the farming community.
